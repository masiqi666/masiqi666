The design processModern Mongolian clothing

A few weeks ago, I went to some of the Mongolian activity, this week began sketching and moved to the clothes, the white t-shirts is the background color, I’m ready to part will be filled into green, part of the filling into blue, it’s just the idea, about in the days after another upload some creation process.


I did some sketches today, but I was not very satisfied with them. Some pigments are not quite complete. I am going to mix some pigments.


The line of the collar refers to the characteristics of Some Mongolian clothes, and two false buttons are drawn on the T-shirt collar after my own modification. The collar will use light yellow as the background colour first, and the second colour will be applied after the colour is dry.


The main reason is to highlight the characteristics of the Mongolian nationality in modern clothes. The Mongolian people are known as the ethnic group on horseback. So I drew a bunch of galloping horses in the lower left corner of my dress.

I coloured the horse black and brown because when I went to the RACES, most of the horses were brown and looked very nice in the sun.


The horse in the back is a pony, and The reason I drew this pony is because I wanted to show that horses have very warm personalities, but also very gentle ones. When I go riding, I can feel some of the horses’ emotions change.


I added purple on the basis of goose yellow, which is softer and brighter. I cut off the sleeves on one side and saw the herdsmen wearing sheepskin vests when they were herding sheep, so I combined tradition with modernity.


I cut tassels and round holes in the waist and shoulders because I wanted to show a bit of randomness in regular clothes, because modern clothes now have these design patterns. Through my own modification, I carried on some traditional and modern clothing characteristics of the collision.


The most famous totem in Mongolia is the Wolf totem. I mainly wanted to draw the Wolf’s eyes, and I used black and white for the main colours. I didn’t draw all of them, because I think one eye can reflect the fierce look in the eyes.


In front of the clothes, I sewed a patch, because, now the characteristics of clothing is very popular patch style.


I cut off my back and left two tassels and tied them together. Since there is a belt in the traditional Mongolian robe, I made a change to use the belt on the back as a body retainer.
